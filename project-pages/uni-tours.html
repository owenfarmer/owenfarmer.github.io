Unicycle Tours

Over the years I've been on a few unicycle tours. I bring a tent, sleeping bag, and supplies with me and I ride 15-30miles per day. I am always self supported but sometimes stop at stores/water wells for supplies. I like it because it is such a unique way to explpore different parts of the world, and the people I meet are always curious and willing to help out.

100 Miles across Montana

This was a quick 3 day tour with my friend Bridger, who also rode on a unicycle. We started at the Canadian border and rode south through Montana. While I only joined him for the first 3 days, he rode all the way to Wyoming.

300 Miles around Hawaii

This tour took me and Paolo around the coast of the Big Island of Hawaii. We rode ~30mi/day for 2 weeks, mostly sticking to the coast. We camped on beaches, parks, and under bridges along the way. One of my favorite parts of this trip was the abundant tropical fruit waiting for us on the sides of the roads. This photo is from us descending into the beautiful Waipio valley.

350 Miles down the Californian coast

I did this tour in the beginning of 2022, starting from Monterey and making my way to LA. It took me a bit over 2 weeks, and I did this tour entirely solo. This was a beautiful route and the state parks along the coast made for easy camping options, although I did my fair share of stealth camping as well. I met so many interesting people and fellow travelers on this trip. When I reached roughly the midway point the beaches started to become more rideable, so I found myself cruising down the sand instead of on the side of the road. This photo was taken from Morro Bay.

100 Miles across Finland & Estonia

I went on this trip in the summer of 2022 with my friend Alexei, who joined me in his flip flops and beach cruiser. We rode through the Finnish countryside and then took a ferry into Tallin where we continued our Estonian portion of the trip.

Munipacking in Southern France

This trip didn't necessecarily have an A to B route that I took. Instead we explored the French Alps taking busses and hitchhiking in our quest to find technical and challenging downhill trails to ride. Every morning we stashed our camping supplies down in the valleys and would figure out a way to make our way up the mountains.

100 Miles in Northern Minnesota

Inspired by my trip to France I wanted to take my smaller wheel and ride some more technical terrain on this tour. I also packed an inflatable raft with me and used it to enjoy the many lakes of Minnesota. What made this trip especially challenging was how remote it was in the area, and I was only able to fill up my water and buy food once on my journey.
