Car Bed Frames

I like to go on road trips around North America in my trusty Toyota Yaris. To keep costs down I've slept various beds that I built in my car.

Car Bed # 1

Lots of storage

On this trip I spent a lot of time in the Southwest. This is the car near Sedona (with 2 unicycles stored underneath!). The first variation of the car bed prioritizes space, since I took this on a 2 month trip and wanted space to store all my gear. My head rests on the dash where the windshield is and my feet brush up against the rear windshield.

I took out the passenger seat from my car to make room for the platform. To determine the shape of the platform, I used cardboard and marked out where the B pillar of the car would be, as well as the shape of the dashboard and A pillar.

I made 2 rectangular frames to rest the platform on. These are the 2 boards cut to size.

This is a photo my friend took of me sleeping outside his apartment in Toronto. As you can see, it is a bit cramped but it gets the job done.

My living room & kitchen for the night in Pennsylvania.

Car Bed # 2

Comfort & Convenience

In this build I wanted to prioritize comfort. I removed more of the backseat to mount the bed platform as low to the ground as possible. Now I was able to sit up in bed, and entrance and exit from the door or the driver's seat was much easier. I still had a bit of storage underneath the bed as well.

Testing out some boards for the platform. I removed all of the backseat cushions to provide one flat surface, and my feet stick into the trunk.

I replaced the cushions with these wooden boards. This is the car in passenger mode, where the middle section of the bed platform serves as the passenger backrest (the OSB behind the seat belts shown here). When its in bed mode, I take the board out and put it back in the bed. No fasteners are needed because the board rests on the lip of the seats, and the contour from the wheel well locks into a corresponding curve on the front bed board.

Car Bed # 3

Queen Mattress in a Yaris

I wanted space for 2 to sleep in this iteration since my girlfrined would be joining me on a trip from Arkansas. So I took out the last backrest and had both of our feet stick into the trunk.
