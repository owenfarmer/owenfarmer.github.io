Mountain Unicycling Weekends

One of my favorite parts about the sport is attending these mountain unicycle (muni) weekends. They take place all over the world in places with some of the most technical and scenic trails. Its always a good time to get together with friends and to hang out with some people also interested in riding.

Fruita CO

I've been to Fruita 4 times now, and my all time favorite trail is in this area. There's one feature I've tried over and over but still have never been able to land. Maybe I'll have to get it on trip #5...

CA Muni Weekend

I've been to the California muni weekend twice now. This photo was taken during the 2020 installment when Cedar and Akira hosted us in the woods near Yosemite in Eastern California. In 2022 Corbin put on the weekend and we rode his local trails near Lake Tahoe.

STOMP

STOMP is an event short for Southern Trails of Oak Mountain Park located in Alabama right outside of Birmingham. There's always a huge crowd for this event and we camp together - always a ton of fun!

Arizona Muni Weekend

I've been to this event 3 times now, and we ride the trails near Phoenix and Sedona. There's a great group of locals in the Arizona area and they ride some of the hardest trails I've seen in the country. This photo is from the infamous white line in Sedona which I rode a portion of (I chickened out at the turnaround).

Curt Gowdy Muni Weekend

This is an event in Wyoming that I went to in 2023. Good mix of flow and super technical, plus love the Front Range uni guys.

Tennessee Muni Weekend

This is an event in I put together in Eastern Tennessee. We rode the trails at Windrock Bike Park for some extremely steep and loose challenges.

Minnesota Muni Weekend

I also put on this event in Duluth, which I've since crowned my favorite Midwestern city. I loved the trails here and all the challenging rocky features and city/Lake Superior views.

Michigan Muni Weekend

In this final installment of my summer muni weekend series, we went up to Michigan's UP to ride some of the hardest trails in the area. We saw some features that nobody even attempted, including one line that went down what was basically a cliff. Along lake superior, the town of Copper Harbor itself was beautiful and fun to explore (all 2 blocks of it).
