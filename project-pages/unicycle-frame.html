Unicycle Frame with attached seat and handlebar

Design

In most unicycles on the market, the handlebar is attached to the end of the seat, which is attached to the frame via the seatpost. Since the seat is made of plastic, when the rider tugs on the handlebar for big jumps, the handlebar deflects. My frame solves this problem by having the frame, seat, and handlebar be one piece.

This unicycle also gives the rider a more ergonomic position for reaching the brake lever.

I went with rectangular tubing to make aligning a brake caliper mount easier. In order to prevent the sharp corners of the tube from hitting the rider knees, I added this slanted portion near the fork.
